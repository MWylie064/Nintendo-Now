The History of Nintendo
  • Humble Beginnings

    On September 23rd, 1889, Fusajir≈ç Yamauchi founded "Nintendo Koppai," a small Japanese business that specialized in selling Hanafuda cards. After awhile, the cards became very popular among all demographics in Japan. As such, Yamauchi expanded his blosoming company by hiring more workers and establishing his headquarters in Kyoto.

    1889

  • Venturing into New Industries

    From 1963 to 1968, the ever-growing company began to experiment in other fields, thanks to their new large quantity of capital. As a result, Nintendo launched a taxi service, hotel chain, food company, and various other products, but they all failed. On the other hand, their toymaking attempts ended up being successful, for the time being.

    1963

  • Struggling as a Toy Company

    By this time, the popularity of playing cards was dwindling, and Nintendo struggled to compete with the larger Japanese toy manufacturers. However, thanks to maintenance engineer Gunpei Yokoi, Nintendo found massive success in the Ultra Hand product, an "extendable arm" toy. Following that sudden success, the business propelled to new heights with similarly inventive ideas.

    1966

  • After assisting with the first commercially available video game console, the Magnavox Odyssey, Nintendo decided to attempt to create their own dedicated console. Finally, in 1977, the company released the Color-TV Game series of devices.

    1977
